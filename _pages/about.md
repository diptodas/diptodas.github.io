I am a Postdoctoral Associate in the [Bowers College of Computing and Information Science](https://bowers.cornell.edu/) at [Cornell University](https://www.cornell.edu/), where I work with [Dr. Aditya Vashistha](https://www.adityavashistha.com/) as part of the [Cornell Global AI Initiative](https://globalai.ai.cornell.edu/). My research examines how AI and algorithmic systems shape decision-making, public discourse, identity, and access to information across varied institutional and cultural contexts. I study how bias and accountability gaps emerge not only from models and datasets but also from the broader infrastructures through which AI is developed, deployed, evaluated, and governed. These infrastructures include institutional documentation, impact assessments, procurement arrangements, benchmarks, low-resource technological ecosystems, and community practices across global contexts. I am particularly interested in cases where assumptions embedded in AI systems and governance mechanisms do not adequately reflect local conditions, knowledge, and experiences.

My research develops through three interconnected threads. The first, **governing AI**, examines policies and infrastructures for making AI accountable and contestable, including public AI registers, impact assessments, procurement practices, and algorithmic decision-making in governmental departments and agencies in areas such as immigration, social services, and education. The second, **evaluating AI**, develops context-sensitive approaches to benchmarking fairness and bias in language technologies and generative AI, particularly for historically marginalized identities and low-resource languages. The third, **situating AI**, investigates the cultural, social, and political dimensions of AI and digital platforms, including identity work, content moderation, civic participation, diaspora mobilization, and immigrant-led community organizations. Together, these threads explain how AI is governed institutionally, evaluated technically, and experienced and contested within particular social worlds.

Drawing on my interdisciplinary training in computer science, information science, and human-computer interaction, I combine computational audits and experiments, language-resource and benchmark development, interactive AI system development, qualitative interviews, trace ethnography, critical theory, and participatory design. My goal is to advance theory and develop empirically grounded approaches to AI design, evaluation, and governance that improve accountability, contestability, cultural validity, and responsiveness to affected communities. My work regularly appears in top computing venues, such as **CHI, CSCW, FAccT, GROUP, and ICTD**, among others, and has received several Best Paper Honorable Mention Awards. It has also been featured in prominent press and media outlets, such as [The Atlantic](https://www.theatlantic.com/technology/archive/2024/01/quora-tragedy-answer-websites/677062/), [The Prothom Alo](https://www.prothomalo.com/bangladesh/8ml8jqzguh), [ACM Crossroads](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3538548), and [Princeton University Press](https://press.princeton.edu/books/hardcover/9780691255811/digital-culture-shock). My research has been supported by competitive funding from the Institute for Pandemics and the National Science Foundation.

Previously, I worked as a postdoctoral fellow in the [Department of Computer Science](https://web.cs.toronto.edu) (also affiliated with the [iSchool](https://ischool.utoronto.ca/)) with [Dr. Syed Ishtiaque Ahmed](https://www.ishtiaque.net/) and [Dr. Shion Guha](https://shionguha.ca/) at the [University of Toronto](https://www.utoronto.ca/). I also have experience with independent industry consulting and over eight years of teaching experience (three and half years as instructor of record) across universities in the US, Canada, and Bangladesh. I completed my PhD and MS in Information Science from the [University of Colorado Boulder](https://www.colorado.edu/), where I worked with [Dr. Bryan Semaan](https://www.colorado.edu/faculty/semaan/). Previously, I obtained an MS in Computer Science from [Missouri State University](https://www.missouristate.edu/) and a BSEngg in Computer Science and Engineering from [Bangladesh University of Engineering & Technology](http://www.buet.ac.bd/).
