## Overview

This project examines how diaspora and immigrant communities engage with digital and financial systems across borders. It focuses on how people use platforms, remittance systems, and organizational tools to support communities back home, navigate institutional barriers, and participate in collective action.

Across different contexts, this work shows how these systems shape what communities can do—while also highlighting how people adapt, resist, and repurpose them in practice.

## Approach

This research combines qualitative and system-level analysis across multiple contexts:

- **Qualitative studies**: Interviews with diaspora members and immigrant nonprofit organizers across North America, Europe, and the Middle East  
- **Organizational analysis**: Examination of how nonprofit groups coordinate work, manage resources, and build trust  
- **Infrastructure analysis**: Study of platforms, remittance systems, and public-sector technologies that shape participation and access  

## Key Findings

- Diasporas are not just supporters—they actively **organize, coordinate, and influence events across borders**  
- Many systems are not designed for immigrant communities, creating barriers around **access, legitimacy, and visibility**  
- Nonprofits often rely on **informal tools and workarounds** to operate under constraints  
- Financial and digital systems can become tools for **collective action**, such as coordinating remittances or boycotts  
- New technologies like AI can both **support and complicate** nonprofit and community work  

## Contributions

- Shows how diasporas act as **active participants in social and political change**, not just distant observers  
- Highlights how infrastructure shapes everyday practices of **care, coordination, and organizing**  
- Introduces concepts such as **diasporic superposition** and **residual mobilities** to explain how people navigate these systems  
- Offers insights for designing **more inclusive and flexible technologies** that better support transnational communities
